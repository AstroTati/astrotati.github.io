# Journey before destination
**Tatiana M. Rodriguez**


I would find it very hard to believe if you say the night sky did not amaze you as a kid, wishing upon a shooting star and being a bit scared when you found the moon following you is probably one of the few universal experiences. I was not the exception, but I did go a step further. Another thing to know about me, I like tidying up, bringing order to chaos is my kind of meditation. Looking at the stars wasn't enough to me, I needed to know and understand exactly what I was looking at, so I decided to become an **Astronomer**.
\
\
It was a long and difficult 6 years, pulling all nighters after work (I was a terrible waitress by the way) to prepare for midterms, stressing over each final oral exam, struggling to find a specialization and a mentor, but I finally got my Master's degree in Astronomy. Unfortunately, it was extremely untimely: the Argentinean government at the time cut the funds for science and education and I was ejected from the system and my country. Fortunately, I am also quite stubborn.
\
\
That's how I got to the USA, the town of Socorro in New Mexico to be precise, where I spent 5 years pursuing a **Ph.D. in Physics**. Studying and doing science was the easy part actually for me, like I said, I am very structured and resolute, adapting to this completely different society and pushing through virtually alone was one of the hardest things I have ever had to face. And then COVID hit only 6 months into my program. Kudos here to my two advisors, Peter and Emmanuel, and my collaboratos Esteban and Anuj, I can confidently say it would have taken me many more years and tears to get to the finish line without your guidance, patience, empathy, and love. 
\
\
In the New Mexican desert I became an expert in radio astrophysicist, but I really missed being able to work with a team since I was the only student in the department working on my research topic (high-mass star formation). Therefore, I decided to continue my career as a postdoctoral researcher in Europe, where the scientific community always seemed more tightknit and collaborative than in the vast United States. I am now working at the University of Cologne, and after experiencing how it is like to work in three very different countries, I realised that academia is not where I see my future self. Don't get me wrong, I am still in love with the night sky, my eyes light up when someone asks me anything about the universe, but I need to look ahead instead of up.
I am now looking to go back to basics, I am rediscovering myself, my skills, and my strengths. 
\
\
And you know what? I wouldn't change a single thing in the path that brought to where I am, my journey has really been the best, even at its worst. I am still looking for the destination, I will let you know when I find it (:
